Actually...

2 cups flour
1 teaspoon baking soda
1/2 teaspoon salt

1 cup unsalted butter (aka 2 sticks) (SLDFJKSKDJFHSKD DO NOT MICROWAVE THE BUTTER. I recommend taking the butter out to sit for about 1/2-1 hour before using it. Leave it outside in the sun, mash it with a mixer, do ANYTHING but microwave it.)
1 cup golden brown sugar
1/2 cup granulated sugar

1 egg
1 teaspoon vanilla

6 ox. semisweet chocolate chips
1 cup toasted almonds (I just go to Berkeley Bowl or something and buy slivered almonds and stick em in the toaster oven :3)

1. Mix flour, baking soda, and salt, set aside.

2. In large bowl, cream butter and sugars until light.

3. Beat in egg and vanilla until light and fluffy.

4. Stir in flour mixture, chocolate chips and almonds. (I sometimes stick the chocolate on after I've spread the dough, but you don't have to.)

5. Scoop out dough onto baking sheet.

6. Flatten cookies with the palm of your hand or a fork that has been dipped in water. (I just spread them out with my finger. Be careful though - if you make them too thin they'll become brittle and burn, and refuse to come off the pan. Too thick, and the inside will still be soft and mushy. I just know that if you can see the pan through the dough then you've spread it too thin.)

7. Bake at 350F for 6-8 minutes or until flat and browned. (I actually do it more like 9-12 minutes, but however your oven works)

8. Cool on cookie sheets. Store airtight.
