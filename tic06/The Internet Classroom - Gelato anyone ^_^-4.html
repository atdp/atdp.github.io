2006/07/17 18:05:55 PDT by Raikia

If we walk quickly, we CAN get there in 10 minutes. Siu-Yuan and I walked pretty leisurely, so we arrived after about 12, maybe 13 or 14 minutes.

Now, about the other stands - IGNORE THE CHOCOLATE. Gelato is enough sugar, and it's about 28346x cheaper. Gelato is $3.75 for 2 small scoops (you can choose 2 different flavors). I forgot how much the large was. Methinks it was $5.25 or $5.75. You get the point. It is a -little- expensive, but well worth it. I don't recommend taking home a pint to share with the family unless you have a ride. It melts slower than regular ice cream, but melted ice cream frozen again just ruins everything.

There's some very nice pizza - caramalized onion with black olives (which I had today), for example. I also remember something about zucchini and/or eggplant. Very unusual and delicious. A very hungry person could eat one alone, or two can share. Served fresh from the oven...which is either wood or stone, it says both. About $5-7.

There's also soup - mild and warm. They have anything from cornbread to chicken-noodle to vegetarian. Once again, about $5-7. (They also give out samples 8D)

The sushi is ridiculously expensive compared to the pizza and soup - HOWEVER. Sushi is the only cold food other than ice cream served in that place. Expensive as it is, they come in beautiful little boxes, and it's surprisingly fresh. Cheapest I saw was a salmon-skin plate, $5.50 or something. It jumps anywhere from $6-12, or somewhere around there. Chopsticks included.
