A carburetor is responsible for combining both air and fuel and sending this air/fuel mixture into a car's engine to be burned, a process that creates engine power. A faulty or malfunctioning carburetor can lead to a variety of engine problems, most of which are related directly to problems with air and fuel delivery into an engine.

Black Exhaust Smoke
1. A car that emits black, or dark, exhaust smoke from its tail pipe is normally a car experiencing a carburetion problem. A carburetor is responsible for balancing out a car's air/fuel mixture, a mixture that flows into a car's engine and gets ignited. If a carburetor allows too much fuel and not enough air into a car's engine, black, sooty exhaust smoke is normally the result.
Hard Starting Condition
2. A car that cranks but fails to start is often times a sign of a carburetor problem. If a carburetor choke, which helps to start a cold engine, gets stuck open or fails to adequately open during initial starting, a car will not start or be very hard to start. A stuck carburetor choke is a common problem preventing a car from being started.
Stuck Engine Idle
3. Normally, a car's engine idle varies depending on the operating temperature of the engine. When the engine is cold, the idle is higher to facilitate engine warm-up; when warm, the idle drops. A car that has an idle that gets stuck at one speed (normally on high idle) and fails to change is usually a sign of a carburetion problem, generally a problem with the carburetor idle solenoid.
Rough Engine Idle
4. A malfunctioning carburetor can negatively impact a car's engine idle. By interfering with engine air/ fuel mixture, engine idle speed, and fuel flow, a carburetor problem can cause a car's engine idle to become choppy and irregular and, in many instances, cause a car to shut off.
Engine Missing
5. An engine miss, or misfire, can often times because by a faulty or malfunctioning carburetor. An engine miss is usually caused by faulty or irregular engine combustion. If a carburetor impedes or restricts air and/or fuel flow into an engine's cylinders, the resulting engine combustion will be compromised, a situation that can cause an engine miss.
